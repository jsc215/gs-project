A Few Thoughts on the Assignment...

I thought this was interesting because the process was backwards in a way. Here are some components, what can you do with them? Usually, one shouldn't just reach for a drop-down, or sweet new progress bar just because it looks cool. Right? That being said, I do like the emphasis that the project put on creativity and intuition and 'figuring something out.'

The restriction of using the components combined with the freedom to do whatever you want with them is a great a way to see a variety of skill sets that go further than technical knowledge.

I started with the backend. Created the express server, then the models, followed by the api endpoints. I use Postman to check the requests. It's great for creating collections that allow you to check against both local and production environments.

On the front end, Material components were awesome but primeNG is incompatible with Angular 6 and also the latest rxjs version. I don't know if that has come up but I found a fix today, a little late by importing the CSS into the global styles sheet. JUst an FYI.

Thank you for a great learning experience and I look forward to feedback and/or discussion of the project.
